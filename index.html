Scenario 1 · Solo Owner-Operator
1 property · 10 units · PM is also the owner.
A landlord who self-manages their own building. All rent from 10 units flows into their DoorLoop Wallet. Since there's no separate owner to pay out — the PM is the owner — they simply withdraw funds to their own bank account when ready.

Scenario 2 · Small Portfolio Property Manager
Multiple properties · Two Wallets · Owners, vendors, and their own account.
A PM managing properties across two ownership structures — a pooled portfolio and a separately banked Property LLC. Each has its own DoorLoop Wallet. Rent from all units flows in; distributions go to property owners, vendors, and the PM's operating account. Vendor card payments are a near-future improvement.

Scenario 3 · Mid-Market Portfolio Property Management Company
377+ units · 5+ Wallets · Distributions at scale.
A large property management company running hundreds of units across multiple sub-portfolios, each with its own DoorLoop Wallet. Rent from all properties is collected and routed to the correct Wallet automatically. The PM manages distributions to dozens of owners and schedules vendor payments — with Card Issuing as the next step to eliminate manual pay runs entirely.
